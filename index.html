Child Labour

Child labour is the practice of employing children below the age of 18 in hazardous and exploitative conditions. It denies them their basic rights to education, health, and development, and perpetuates poverty. It is a global issue that requires collective efforts to address and eradicate.

Child Labour Working at Construction

Child labour working in construction involves children being employed in dangerous and physically demanding tasks, often for long hours and little pay. This deprives them of their right to education and exposes them to serious health risks. It is a form of exploitation that needs to be addressed urgently.

Working at Leather Factory.

Working in leather factories involves children handling toxic chemicals, operating heavy machinery, and performing physically demanding tasks for long hours. This deprives them of their right to education and exposes them to serious health risks. It is a form of child exploitation that needs to be eradicated.

    What is child labour?

    `Child labour is a global issue that prevents children from fulfilling their potential. Child labour is a violation of children’s rights – the work can harm them mentally or physically, expose them to hazardous situations or stop them from going to school

    • Around the world, millions of children do work in child labour
    • They work in fields, in factories, down mines, as servants or maids, or selling goods in the street or at markets
    • Girls are more likely than boys to do domestic work, such as cleaning, making food and serving

    The International Labour Organization said in 2017 that about 152 million children aged from five to 17 – 64 million girls and 88 million girls – are engaged in child labour.
